Wisdom Teeth Extraction:
Safe, Comfortable Care for Your Smile

Wisdom teeth are the third set of molars that typically emerge between the ages of 17 and 25. Due to their late appearance, there's often not enough room in the mouth for them to grow properly, leading to potential complications.

Common Wisdom Teeth Problems:

Damage to Neighboring Teeth

Wisdom teeth can push against adjacent teeth, causing discomfort or even damage.

Gum Infection

The partially erupted wisdom tooth can cause gum irritation and infections.

Jaw Infections

If wisdom teeth are impacted, they can lead to painful jaw infections.

Alignment Disruption

Impacted wisdom teeth may shift other teeth, affecting the alignment of your smile.

While not all wisdom teeth require removal, if any of the above issues arise, extraction may be necessary to avoid further complications and preserve your oral health.
